About the programmers who made this site.

John

John Grillo has been developing websites and web applications since the mid 90's. “Back then, Space Jam was quite literally the coolest website ever. It's an internet relic, now, but my god did it inspire me...to be better so I could charge 3x as much as whatever the WB paid that guy for.” John Grillo has ample experience in JavaScript (and libraries including jQuery, Angular and node.js), HTML5, CSS3 as well as various flavors of SQL. “MSSQL/TSQL is my favorite. It is more stable than MySQL.” John Grillo is an alumni of Pacific University in Forest Grove and has been enrolled in PSU and PCC as well. “I'm taking CodeFellows as a fun refresher course. I have to say that there have been some surprisingly challenging aspects of it. I'm impressed. My favorite project to date has to be the KidsMathGame. I can think of no better way of rebuilding a portfolio, if you have to, than taking a software developer's bootcamp.” John Grillo is an Eagle Scout in the Boy Scouts of America. “My project was using natural predators to control the mosquito population. Mosquitos love lactic acid and carbon dioxide. Guess who whas those in abundance? Athletes! Hillsboro made a stadium next to sizeable bodies of stagnant water. I knew nothing but I knuckled down, did the research and made sure the predators, swallows [EDIT: It is unclear if the they were Mongolian or European].” When not saving the world, John Grillo likes to spend his time playing basketball, exploring the Pacific NW and working on his short stories and novels. “It's a book of ghost stories for ghosts. It is called “Phantasmagoria: The Dream Pageant”.”

Richard Farr

KidsMathGame is the brain child of Richard Farr. Farr originally conceived of the idea by wanting to make his own game for his son to learn math. “Starting conditions are everything. I wanted to make sure that he learned math the right way. You can't go through life not knowing math.” Farr successfully swayed his CodeFellows 201 teammates of his project by saying that “It's brilliantly simple. Meeting the technical Requirements should be easy and then we can focus on making it go from “good” to “great.”

Richard Farr is an advertising and Google Ad-words master. His favorite t-shirt reads “Rank for show, convert for dough.” He originally took the CodeFellows 201 course with the idea of being able to automate portions of his job. “I love playing with my son, rough-housing and wrestling with him really is my hobby.” Farr's other hobbies are family time, keeping physically fit and cooking.

Richard Farr hails from the town of Bend, Oregon. “I wouldn't want to live anywhere else. Our local dentist won the world championship for paragliding! It isn't just Portland that's wondeful and weird.”
